Preface


When I closed the 10th edition of ANABOLICS seven years ago, I wasn't sure when I'd be working on the book again. By that point I had updated and expanded all of the drug profiles to a place I was happy with. Plus, the field of anabolic steroids is a fairly static one. Not a lot changes with these substances. What I didn't foresee was just how much the market for emerging drugs would grow... how much people would embrace new alternatives to the old basics. Likewise, quite a lot actually has happened in those seven years.This edition is about catching up.

In this new edition of ANABOLICS you'll find twenty new drug profiles. Several of these are SARMs (Selective Androgen Receptor Modulators). For those unfamiliar, this is a new category of agents that act similar to anabolic steroids.They bind the same receptor, and also support increased protein synthesis and muscle growth. However, most of these new drugs are non-steroidal in structure. Further, they have a much stronger separation of anabolic and androgenic effect. While I do not believe these agents are more powerful, risk-free, or poised to unseat steroids as the most common muscle-building drugs out there, I will say that I believe there is something of substance here. Some SARMs have already been in human trials. I suspect we will see a legitimate pharmaceutical product emerge from this category soon.

The category Peptides has seen even more expansion, and is a key focus of this new edition as well. These are largely drugs that stimulate the release of growth hormone, or work through the wider GH axis in some way. We've had one new drug approved in this class already (Egrifta), and several others have entered human clinical trials. I believe in here we may find more than one cost-effective alternative to somatropin (recombinant growth hormone), and possibly a surprising novel anabolic drug or two. As with SARMs, there is still much work to be done before this category matures. For now though, we're digging into the data and empirical evidence with much interest.

Extremely personal to me, the research behind arachidonic acid (X-FACTOR™) took a big step forward in 2016. A second clinical study was published, which found its supplementation to break training stagnation, and induce new size, strength, and power gains in experienced athletes. Arachidonic acid is the body's most bioactive omega-6fattyacid.lt is richly stored in the muscles, and necessary for triggering tissue inflammation and repair after training-induced damage. Its depletion may be a key-contributing factor to declining gains over time. Similarly, its supplementation may be an effective way to trigger new gains. Plateau is an especially common problem with experienced athletes. I believe my work in this area has opened a wide door into our greater understanding and mitigation of it. I encourage you to take a look.

I've also cofounded Colorimetries since the last update.This company manufactures ROIDTEST™ presumptive steroid test kits.These kits verify the active drug in a product with a visual chemical reaction.While the technology cannot determine the dosage yet, it does an incredible job of indicating if there is really oxandrolone in your Anavar, methenolone in your Primobolan, etc. Drug substitution (or outright bunk product) is a major problem these days. The kits are fast, easy, and finally give you the tools to do something about it. I can't emphasize how proud of this I am. I worked closely with the research/production team, and helped facilitate the evolution of this technology to a point where it is exceedingly effective for counterfeit identification. You can learn more about it at www.roidtest.com.

This is also the first edition of ANABOLICS with contributing writers. I invited Willem Koert, Dr. Thomas O'Connor, Jake Shelley, and Dr. George Touliatos to help expand the book with new sections and profiles. Each and all are exceedingly smart and knowledgeable individuals, and added valuable and informed new perspectives to ANABOLICS. I hope you will agree, and will join me in welcoming them to the team.

With that, I thank you whole-heartedly for your trust and support, and welcome you to the 11th Edition of ANABOLICS.


-William Llewellyn

William Llewellyn is a scientist in the field of human performance enhancement. As a former illicit steroid user, his knowledge about anabolic drugs is formed from both real world experience and scientific research. He is eager to share information and experiences with others, and is a longtime supporter of the steroid harm reduction community. William also serves as an honorary lecturerat Liverpool John Moores University, assisting educational efforts at its Centre for Public Health. He is also a longtime monthly columnist for MUSCULAR DEVELOPMENT magazine, and has contributed to many other industry and media products over the years. He has appeared on the covers of ESPN Magazine and The Washington Post.

As a scientist, William's most notable accomplishment has been discovering the anabolic role of arachidonic acid supplementation in humans.This is the body's most active omega-6 fatty acid.Though widely researched in other areas, its ability to support muscle hypertrophy and mitigate training stagnation had not been characterized before his groundbreaking work.
